Author Uknown

Is this a poem? It seems like a simple question, but the answer may be more complicated than you imagine. If you think Leaves Falling, as I call it, is a poem, how would you defend it? Many are reluctant to accept it into their schema of what a poem is.

Personally, I like to think of poetry as art whose medium is words (whether written or spoken). A loose definition like mine blurs the line between poetry and prose, but in my opinion, the difference is author's intent. Poetry consists of words and is usually organized into lines and stanzas. Lines and stanzas are similar to other writing's sentences and paragraphs, and just like them, their separation is all about meaning. Poetry may include phonetic patterns called rhyme schemes and meter (a convenient example is iambic pentameter, made famous by the Bard himself, William Shakespeare).

One important thing for you to know about poetry is that it is not a genre. Genres do, however, exist within the greater subject of poetry. So do poetic forms: rule-bounded types that have set stanza lengths, rhyme schemes, meter, etc. My personal favorite is the villanelle (check out the Poetry Foundation's glossary for more information - there's a link under "Writing" in the footer).

Performance poetry is a special type of poetry that is written for dramatic reading. Slam and beatnik poetry fall under this category. Performance poetry tends to tackle current events and controversial topics; it is especially popular among teens and young adults.

Poetry is as diverse as a language is versatile - write to your heart's content!
