Great Poets

This page features a few historical poets - new each week! I find that their writings provide insight into the human experience, and inspire us to create something great just like they did.

Maya Angelou

Born in Missouri in 1928, Angelou had a diverse career that took her all over the world. Brushing shoulders with Dr. Martin Luthor Kind, Jr., Malcolm X, and Oprah Winfrey, she stood against racism and sexism throughout her life.

Famous Works
  • Caged Bird
  • Awaking in New York
  • California Prodigal

Edgar Allan Poe

Poe was born in Boston in 1809. Orphaned at a young age, Poe was one of the first American writers to make a living on writing alone. He is recognized for his impact on the short story form and detective fiction as an emerging genre.

Famous Works
  • The Raven
  • "Alone"
  • A Dream Within a Dream

Langston Hughes

Hughes, known particularly for his portrayal of Black life in America, was born in Missouri in 1902. An important artistic figure in the Harlem Renaiisance, his work extended beyond poetry into novels, plays, short stories, and essays.

Famous Works
  • Black Maria
  • Crossing Jordan
  • Blues in Stereo

William Shakespeare

Known primarily for his dramatic plays, Shakespeare was born in 1564 in Stratford-upon-Avon, England. Througout his career, Shakespeare wrote both short and book-length poetry that explored themes such as love, lust, death, and history.

Famous Works
  • Sonnet 18
  • "All the world's a stage"
  • Venus and Adonis

Ralph Waldo Emerson

Emerson was born in Boston in 1809. Having been exposed to international cultures from a young age, he included Asian and Middle Eastern ideas and metaphysical concepts in his writing. Emerson is also recognized for his role in the Transcendalist movement.

Famous Works
  • Brahma
  • Terminus
  • Each and All
